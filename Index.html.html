Policy Implication

The Peak-Peek Problem: Why It Matters

The brief, sharp demand spike ("peak-peek") lasting only 1–3 hours forces DISCOMs to purchase power at ₹9.5–15/unit on spot markets, compared to ₹3–5/unit for scheduled base load. In FY 2024–25, 37–40% of all purchase slots exceeded ₹9.5/unit — almost entirely due to peak-hour purchases.

▲
Managing 100 peak hours/year reduces system peak by 8–10% (CEEW)
₹
Augmentation cost: ₹1.5–2 Cr/MW — potentially avoidable via demand response or BESS
✓
Top 10–12% of capacity used for fewer than 100 hours/year (UPCL LDC data)
CEA Outlook

Peak Demand Trajectory to 2035–36

CEA's National Generation Adequacy Plan (March 2026) projects peak demand to grow from 289 GW (FY26-27) to 459 GW by 2035-36 at a 5.58% CAGR. Energy requirement grows at 6.41% CAGR, reaching 3,365 BU. Growing faster than base load, driven by urbanisation, EV adoption, and cooling loads.

⚡
55–70 GW of 4-hour BESS needed by 2030 to manage RE-peak mismatch (CEEW 2025)

Key Findings from LDC Analysis CEEW Policy Implications
Key Finding 1

Peak Load Rising Faster Than Base Load

LDC data across three years shows the peak end of the curve (top 500–1,000 hours) rising faster than the median load. This widens the "peaking gap" — forcing increasingly expensive spot purchases and system augmentation.

Key Finding 2

Fewer Than 100 Hours Drive Expensive Capacity

The top 1.4% of the year (123 hours in FY23-24) saw demand above 90% of peak capacity. These hours drive peak power plant investment and spot market purchases at ₹9.5–15/unit. Demand response or 4-hour BESS could eliminate most of this cost.

Key Finding 3

Asset Under-Utilisation Across 8,000+ Hours

Distribution infrastructure operates at below 60% capacity for over 22% of the year (Uttarakhand). This structural under-utilisation means capital is deployed inefficiently — the same infrastructure that strains during peak hours sits largely idle for months.

Research Gap

Replicating This Analysis Across All States

Replicating the UPCL LDC analysis for all 28 states requires: (1) SLDC half-hourly demand data — available via POSOCO/NLDC public reports; (2) State-level installed capacity — PFC Annual Report Ch. 3; (3) Cross-validation with MoP Integrated Rating. Most SLDCs publish daily demand curves; automated aggregation is technically feasible.

Reform Programmes & Policy Initiatives

Key Central Government reform schemes for the power distribution sector. Status and outcomes as of FY 2024–25.

Source: MoP PIB · PFC Nodal Agency Reports
Active Scheme

RDSS — Revamped Distribution Sector Scheme

Launched July 2021. Total outlay ₹3,03,758 Cr (GBS: ₹97,631 Cr). Duration: FY 2021–22 to FY 2025–26. Nodal agencies: PFC and REC Limited. Target: AT&C losses to 12–15%, ACS-ARR gap to zero by FY 2025–26.

📊
48 DISCOMs across 30 States/UTs participating
🔌
Infrastructure works sanctioned: ₹1.22 lakh Cr · Smart metering: ₹1.30 lakh Cr
📱
Smart meters sanctioned: 19+ crore · Physical progress: 17% (MoP 2024)
Source: MoP PIB · PFC Annual Report · Lok Sabha reply Nov 2024
In Force

LPS Rules 2022 — Late Payment Surcharge

Electricity (Late Payment Surcharge and Related Matters) Rules, 2022. DISCOMs must pay Gencos within 45 days or face LPS. Legacy dues converted to EMIs (max 48 months). Compliance linked to RDSS fund release.

▼
Dues reduced 96%: ₹1,39,947 Cr (Jun 2022) → ₹4,927 Cr (Jan 2026) · PIB 2025
📅
Payment cycle: 178 days (FY21) → 113 days (FY25) · PIB PRID 2215761
⚡
Non-compliance: restricted exchange access + reduced PPA drawl (10%/30 days)
Source: PIB PRID 2215761 · PRAAPTI Portal · Business Standard
Historical

UDAY — Ujwal DISCOM Assurance Yojana (FY16–FY20)

States took over 75% of DISCOM debt, converting it to state government bonds. Provided temporary financial relief. AT&C loss reduction and tariff revision targets were largely missed. Accumulated losses re-built post-scheme.

⚠
None of the DISCOMs achieved AT&C loss targets during 2015–21 (CAG Report 2024)
⚠
Rajasthan: AT&C losses surpassed pre-UDAY levels by 2018-21 (CAG Audit)
📋
Lessons shaped RDSS design: results-linked funding, audited accounts prerequisite
Source: CAG Report No.1 (2024) · MoP Annual Report
Sector Target

National Electricity Plan 2023–32 (NEP)

CEA's National Electricity Plan projects peak demand reaching 458 GW by 2031-32. Transmission network to expand from 4.98 lakh ckm to 6.48 lakh ckm. Total plan cost ₹9.16 lakh Cr. Includes 500 GW non-fossil capacity target by 2029-30.
