For Beginners
What exactly is a DISCOM?
A DISCOM (Distribution Company) is the company that delivers electricity to your home, office, or factory. Think of the power sector like a supply chain: power plants generate electricity → transmission lines carry it across long distances → DISCOMs distribute it to you and collect your bill. India has 72 such utilities, mostly state-owned. Their financial health directly determines whether you get reliable power — and whether your tariff goes up.
⚡
AT&C Loss
Aggregate Technical & Commercial loss. Simply: electricity that was produced but not paid for. This includes power lost in wires AND power consumed but not billed/collected. National target is 12–15%.
National avg: 15.04% ✓
📊
ACS–ARR Gap
Average Cost of Supply minus Average Revenue Realised. If it costs ₹7 to supply 1 unit but you only recover ₹6.61, the gap is ₹0.39/unit. That shortfall is covered by state government subsidies or leads to losses.
Govt target: Zero gap
🏦
Accumulated Losses
The total debt pile built up over decades of losses. Even though DISCOMs posted their first profit in FY25, the ₹6.47 lakh crore in accumulated losses is effectively guaranteed by state governments — affecting their credit ratings.
Still a systemic risk

How is the Stress Score calculated?
Each utility is scored 0–100 based on four factors: AT&C loss level (how much power is lost/unbilled), ACS–ARR gap (how much they lose per unit sold), trend direction (getting better or worse?), and collection efficiency. A score above 60 means the utility is at significant risk of payment defaults or supply disruption.
